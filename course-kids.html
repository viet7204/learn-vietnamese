🌈 Phase 1: Hello Vietnam! (Weeks 1-4)
Week 1

👋 Hello! Xin Chào!

Greetings and introducing yourself

What Kids Will Learn:

  • Greetings: Xin chào (Hello), Tạm biệt (Goodbye), Cảm ơn (Thank you)
  • Self-introduction: "My name is..." (Tên tôi là...)
  • Simple questions: "What's your name?" (Bạn tên gì?)
  • Polite words: Please, Thank you, Sorry

Fun Activities This Week:

🎮 GAME Hello Musical Chairs

Kids walk around. When music stops, they greet the nearest person in Vietnamese!

🎵 SONG "Xin Chào" Song

Tune: "Hello Song"
"Xin chào, xin chào, bạn khỏe không?
Tôi khỏe, tôi khỏe, cảm ơn bạn!"

(Hello, hello, how are you? I'm fine, I'm fine, thank you!)

🎨 CRAFT Name Tag Decoration

Kids create colorful name tags with their Vietnamese names and decorate with Vietnamese flag colors!

📖 STORY "My First Day in Vietnam"

Picture book about a child visiting Vietnam for the first time and learning to say hello!

Parent/Teacher Tip:

Practice greetings during daily routines! Say "Xin chào!" in the morning and "Tạm biệt!" at bedtime. Make it a fun family habit!

Homework: Teach the "Xin chào" song to a family member or friend!

Downloadable Materials:

📄 Flashcards (Greetings) 🎨 Coloring Sheet 🎵 Song Lyrics + Audio 📝 Parent Guide
Week 2

🌈 Colors & Numbers 1-10

Exploring the rainbow and counting

What Kids Will Learn:

  • Colors: Đỏ (Red), Xanh (Blue/Green), Vàng (Yellow), Hồng (Pink), Trắng (White), Đen (Black)
  • Numbers 1-10: Một, Hai, Ba, Bốn, Năm, Sáu, Bảy, Tám, Chín, Mười
  • Simple phrases: "I like..." (Tôi thích...)

Fun Activities This Week:

🎮 GAME Color Scavenger Hunt

Kids find objects of different colors around the room and say the color in Vietnamese!

🎵 SONG "Rainbow Colors" (Cầu Vồng)

Tune: "Twinkle Twinkle Little Star"
"Đỏ, vàng, xanh, and hồng too,
Colors in the rainbow, bright and new!"

🎨 CRAFT Number Finger Puppets

Create finger puppets numbered 1-10 in Vietnamese. Use them to practice counting!

📖 STORY "The Rainbow Dragon"

A friendly dragon loses its colors and needs help finding them. Kids help by naming each color in Vietnamese!

Parent/Teacher Tip:

Count everything together in Vietnamese! Steps, toys, snacks, fingers... Make it a daily game. Point to objects and ask "What color?" in Vietnamese.

Homework: Count 10 toys at home and say the color of each one in Vietnamese!

Downloadable Materials:

🎴 Color Flashcards 🔢 Number Cards 🎨 Rainbow Worksheet 🎵 Counting Song Audio
Week 3

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 My Family (Gia Đình)

Learning about family members

What Kids Will Learn:

  • Family members: Bố/Ba (Dad), Mẹ/Má (Mom), Anh (Older brother), Chị (Older sister), Em (Younger sibling)
  • Grandparents: Ông (Grandpa), Bà (Grandma)
  • Phrases: "This is my..." (Đây là... của tôi)

Fun Activities This Week:

🎮 GAME Family Photo Show & Tell

Kids bring family photos and introduce their family members in Vietnamese!

🎵 SONG "My Family Song"

Tune: "The Finger Family"
"Bố, bố, where are you? Here I am, here I am!
Mẹ, mẹ, where are you? Here I am, here I am!"

🎨 CRAFT Family Tree Art Project

Create a colorful family tree with Vietnamese labels for each family member!

📖 STORY "A Vietnamese Family Dinner"

Story about a family gathering where each member is introduced. Learn Vietnamese family terms naturally!

Parent/Teacher Tip:

Vietnamese has special pronouns for family members! Even if simplified for kids, introduce the concept that Vietnam shows respect through language. Practice with extended family if possible!

Homework: Draw your family and label everyone in Vietnamese!

Downloadable Materials:

👨‍👩‍👧 Family Flashcards 🌳 Family Tree Template 🎵 Family Song Audio 📝 Family Worksheet
Week 4

🐶 Animals (Động Vật)

Exploring the animal kingdom

What Kids Will Learn:

  • Common animals: Chó (Dog), Mèo (Cat), Chim (Bird), Voi (Elephant), Khỉ (Monkey), Cá (Fish)
  • Animal sounds: Vietnamese animal onomatopoeia!
  • Questions: "What animal is this?" (Động vật này là gì?)

Fun Activities This Week:

🎮 GAME Animal Charades

Kids act out animals while others guess in Vietnamese. Make animal sounds together!

🎵 SONG "Old MacDonald's Farm" (Vietnamese Version)

"Old MacDonald had a farm... with a chó chó here..."
Learn animal sounds in Vietnamese - fun fact: They're different from English!

🎨 CRAFT Paper Plate Animal Masks

Create masks of favorite Vietnamese animals. Wear them while practicing animal names!

📖 STORY "The Water Buffalo and the Tiger"

Traditional Vietnamese folktale featuring animals. Learn why the water buffalo is so important in Vietnam!

Parent/Teacher Tip:

Visit a zoo or watch animal videos together. Practice animal names and sounds. Vietnamese animal sounds are different - dogs say "gâu gâu" not "woof woof"!

Homework: Find pictures of 5 animals and label them in Vietnamese!

Downloadable Materials:

🦁 Animal Flashcards 🎭 Mask Templates 🎵 Farm Song Audio 📚 Folktale PDF
🏠 Phase 2: Daily Life (Weeks 5-8)
Week 5

🍜 Yummy Food (Đồ Ăn)

Vietnamese food and eating together

What Kids Will Learn:

  • Food words: Cơm (Rice), Phở (Pho soup), Bánh mì (Bread/Sandwich), Nước (Water), Trái cây (Fruit)
  • Eating phrases: "I'm hungry" (Tôi đói bụng), "Delicious!" (Ngon!)
  • Manners: Table etiquette in Vietnamese culture

Fun Activities This Week:

🎮 GAME Vietnamese Restaurant Role-Play

Set up a pretend Vietnamese restaurant! Kids take turns being customer and waiter, ordering in Vietnamese.

🎵 SONG "Eating Song" (Bài Hát Ăn Cơm)

Tune: "Are You Hungry?"
"Ăn cơm, ăn cơm, ngon lắm! (Eat rice, eat rice, so yummy!)"

🎨 CRAFT Paper Vietnamese Food

Create paper versions of phở, bánh mì, spring rolls using construction paper and cotton balls!

📖 STORY "The Magic Rice Bowl"

Vietnamese folktale about a magic bowl that never empties. Learn about rice's importance in Vietnamese culture!

Parent/Teacher Tip:

Cook simple Vietnamese food together! Try making bánh mì sandwiches or fruit smoothies (sinh tố). Visit a Vietnamese restaurant and practice ordering!

Homework: Try one Vietnamese food this week and describe it in Vietnamese (Ngon! or Không ngon)!

Downloadable Materials:

🍱 Food Flashcards 📋 Menu Template 🎵 Eating Song Audio 👨‍🍳 Simple Recipe Cards
Week 6

👋 Body Parts & Actions

Learn body parts and action verbs

What Kids Will Learn:

  • Body parts: Đầu (Head), Mắt (Eyes), Mũi (Nose), Tai (Ears), Tay (Hand/Arm), Chân (Leg/Foot)
  • Actions: Nhảy (Jump), Chạy (Run), Đi (Walk), Ngồi (Sit), Đứng (Stand)
  • Commands: Simple action commands

Fun Activities This Week:

🎮 GAME Vietnamese Simon Says

"Simon says: Nhảy! (Jump!)" Kids follow commands in Vietnamese. Great for active learning!

🎵 SONG "Head, Shoulders, Knees and Toes" (Vietnamese)

"Đầu, vai, đầu gối, chân..." Touch body parts while singing!

🎨 CRAFT Vietnamese Body Parts Puppet

Create a puppet with movable parts. Label each body part in Vietnamese!

📖 STORY "The Boy Who Could Run Like the Wind"

Story about using your body for sports and activities. Learn action verbs naturally!

Parent/Teacher Tip:

Make exercise time Vietnamese time! Do stretches and exercises while naming body parts and actions in Vietnamese. Dance to Vietnamese children's music!

Homework: Teach someone the "Head, Shoulders, Knees and Toes" song in Vietnamese!

Downloadable Materials:

👤 Body Parts Flashcards 🏃 Action Verb Cards 🎵 Song Lyrics + Audio 🎭 Puppet Template
Week 7

☀️ Weather & Seasons

Talking about weather and seasons

What Kids Will Learn:

  • Weather: Nắng (Sunny), Mưa (Rain), Lạnh (Cold), Nóng (Hot)
  • Seasons: Xuân (Spring), Hạ (Summer), Thu (Fall), Đông (Winter)
  • Questions: "How's the weather?" (Thời tiết thế nào?)

Fun Activities This Week:

🎮 GAME Weather Reporter

Kids pretend to be TV weather reporters! Give daily weather reports in Vietnamese.

🎵 SONG "Rain Rain Go Away" (Vietnamese)

"Mưa mưa đi đi, trở lại ngày khác..."

🎨 CRAFT Weather Wheel

Create a spinning weather wheel with different weather conditions labeled in Vietnamese!

📖 STORY "Monsoon Season in Vietnam"

Learn about Vietnam's unique weather patterns and rainy season!

Parent/Teacher Tip:

Check the weather together each morning in Vietnamese! Keep a weather calendar and mark each day with Vietnamese weather words.

Homework: Track the weather for one week using Vietnamese words!

Downloadable Materials:

🌤️ Weather Flashcards 📅 Weather Calendar 🎵 Weather Song Audio 🎡 Wheel Template
Week 8

👕 Clothes (Quần Áo)

Learning about clothing and traditional Vietnamese dress

What Kids Will Learn:

  • Clothing: Áo (Shirt), Quần (Pants), Váy (Skirt/Dress), Giày (Shoes)
  • Special clothing: Áo dài (Traditional Vietnamese dress)
  • Phrases: "I'm wearing..." (Tôi đang mặc...)

Fun Activities This Week:

🎮 GAME Fashion Show

Kids dress up and describe their outfits in Vietnamese! Learn about áo dài and traditional clothing.

🎵 SONG "Getting Dressed Song"

Tune: "This Is The Way"
"This is the way we put on our áo, áo, áo..."

🎨 CRAFT Design Your Own Áo Dài

Color and decorate templates of traditional Vietnamese áo dài!

📖 STORY "The Beautiful Áo Dài"

Story about a special occasion where everyone wears áo dài. Learn about Vietnamese culture!

Parent/Teacher Tip:

Practice getting dressed in Vietnamese! As kids put on clothes, name each item. Show pictures or videos of áo dài to teach cultural appreciation.

Homework: Draw yourself wearing your favorite outfit and label the clothes in Vietnamese!

Downloadable Materials:

👗 Clothing Flashcards 🎨 Áo Dài Coloring Page 🎵 Dressing Song Audio 📷 Cultural Photos
🎉 Phase 3: Vietnamese Culture & Fun (Weeks 9-12)
Week 9

🎊 Tết - Vietnamese New Year!

Celebrating Vietnam's biggest holiday

What Kids Will Learn:

  • Tết greetings: Chúc mừng năm mới! (Happy New Year!)
  • Traditions: Lucky money (lì xì), Bánh chưng (special cake), Fireworks (pháo hoa)
  • Zodiac animals: Vietnamese zodiac signs

Fun Activities This Week:

🎮 GAME Tết Celebration Party

Host a mini Tết celebration! Give lucky red envelopes, eat special treats, and practice greetings!

🎵 SONG "Tết Đến Tết Đến"

Traditional Vietnamese New Year song for children!

🎨 CRAFT Make Red Envelopes & Peach Blossoms

Create traditional Tết decorations: red envelopes (bao lì xì) and paper peach blossoms!

📖 STORY "The Legend of Bánh Chưng"

Traditional story about why Vietnamese people eat bánh chưng during Tết!

Parent/Teacher Tip:

This is a great week to involve Vietnamese community members! Invite families to share Tết traditions. Try making bánh chưng or bánh tét if possible.

Homework: Learn which Vietnamese zodiac animal represents your birth year!

Downloadable Materials:

🎊 Tết Flashcards 🧧 Red Envelope Template 🎵 Tết Songs Audio 📖 Zodiac Poster
Week 10

🎮 Vietnamese Games & Toys

Traditional games Vietnamese kids play

What Kids Will Learn:

  • Toys: Đồ chơi (Toy), Búp bê (Doll), Bóng (Ball), Diều (Kite)
  • Games: Traditional Vietnamese children's games
  • Phrases: "Let's play!" (Chơi nào!), "My turn!" (Đến lượt tôi!)

Fun Activities This Week:

🎮 GAME Play "Ô Ăn Quan" (Mandarin Square Capturing)

Traditional Vietnamese board game using stones! Learn to count and strategize in Vietnamese.

🎮 GAME "Nhảy Dây" (Jump Rope)

Vietnamese jump rope songs and rhymes! Physical activity + language learning.

🎨 CRAFT Make Vietnamese Kites (Diều)

Create simple paper kites inspired by traditional Vietnamese designs!

📖 STORY "The Kite Festival"

Story about children flying kites during a Vietnamese festival!

Parent/Teacher Tip:

Many traditional games don't need fancy equipment! Teach kids that Vietnamese children play with simple, creative toys. It's a great cultural lesson!

Homework: Teach a friend or family member one Vietnamese game!

Downloadable Materials:

🎲 Game Instructions 🪁 Kite Template 🎵 Jump Rope Rhymes 📝 Toy Vocabulary
Week 11

🗺️ Traveling Vietnam

Exploring Vietnamese geography and landmarks

What Kids Will Learn:

  • Places: Hà Nội (Hanoi), Sài Gòn (Saigon), Biển (Beach), Núi (Mountain)
  • Landmarks: Vịnh Hạ Long (Ha Long Bay), Hội An (Ancient town)
  • Transportation: Xe máy (Motorbike), Xe đạp (Bicycle), Thuyền (Boat)

Fun Activities This Week:

🎮 GAME Vietnam Map Quiz

Interactive game identifying Vietnamese cities and landmarks on a map!

🎵 SONG "Travel Around Vietnam"

Song about traveling from North to South Vietnam, naming cities and foods along the way!

🎨 CRAFT Vietnamese Postcard

Create postcards from famous Vietnamese locations. Write greetings in Vietnamese!

📖 STORY "Journey Through Vietnam"

Picture book touring Vietnam's beautiful landscapes and cities!

Parent/Teacher Tip:

Show videos or photos of Vietnam! Many kids (especially heritage learners) may have family connections. Let them share stories about visiting Vietnam!

Homework: Find one interesting fact about Vietnam to share with the class!

🗺️ Vietnam Map 🏛️ Landmark Cards 🎵 Travel Song Audio 📮 Postcard Template

🌟 Tips for Success! 🌟

✨ Keep It Fun!

Children learn best through play. If they're having fun, they're learning!

🔄 Repetition Is Key

Review previous weeks regularly. Kids need lots of repetition to retain new vocabulary.

🎨 Multiple Modalities

Use visual, auditory, and kinesthetic activities. Different kids learn in different ways!

👨‍👩‍👧 Family Involvement

Encourage practice at home. Share weekly homework that families can do together!

🎉 Celebrate Progress

Praise effort, not just perfection. Every word learned is an achievement!

🌍 Cultural Connection

Connect language to culture. Help kids understand WHY they're learning Vietnamese!
