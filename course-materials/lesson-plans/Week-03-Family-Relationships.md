# Week 3: Family & Relationships
## Lesson Plan - Adult Beginners Course

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Name immediate family members in Vietnamese
2. Describe their family structure and size
3. Use possessive structures (của, của tôi)
4. Count family members using classifiers
5. Understand basic Vietnamese kinship terms
6. Talk about marital status

### Vocabulary (40-50 words)

**Family Members:**
- Gia đình - Family
- Bố/Ba - Father
- Mẹ/Ma - Mother
- Anh trai - Older brother
- Chị gái - Older sister
- Em trai - Younger brother
- Em gái - Younger sister
- Con trai - Son
- Con gái - Daughter
- Ông - Grandfather
- Bà - Grandmother
- Cô - Aunt (father's younger sister)
- Chú - Uncle (father's younger brother)
- Dì - Aunt (mother's younger sister)
- Cậu - Uncle (mother's younger brother)

**Relationships:**
- Vợ - Wife
- Chồng - Husband
- Bạn - Friend
- Người yêu - Boyfriend/Girlfriend

**Descriptors:**
- Lớn - Big/Old
- Nhỏ - Small/Young
- Của - Of (possessive)
- Có - Have/Has
- Người - Person (classifier)

### Grammar Focus

1. **Possessive Structure:**
   - Của + pronoun: của tôi (my/mine), của bạn (your/yours)
   - Direct structure: gia đình tôi (my family)

2. **"Có" (to have):**
   - Tôi có hai em gái (I have two younger sisters)
   - Bạn có anh trai không? (Do you have an older brother?)

3. **Classifiers:**
   - Người (for people): ba người (three people)
   - Classifier goes between number and noun

### Lesson Plan (120 minutes)

#### 1. Warm-up & Review (15 min)

**Activity: "Introduction Speed Round"**
- Students stand in two lines facing each other
- Each student introduces themselves to person opposite
- After 30 seconds, one line shifts over
- Repeat 5 times
- Focus on: Tên tôi là..., Tôi đến từ...

**Vocabulary Quiz:**
- Quick 5-minute quiz on Weeks 1-2 vocabulary (20 items)
- Collect and grade quickly during break

**Materials:** Quiz sheets (prepared)

#### 2. Presentation: Family Vocabulary (20 min)

**Visual Introduction:**
- Display family tree on board/screen
- Introduce immediate family first:
  - Bố (father)
  - Mẹ (mother)
  - Anh trai / Chị gái (older siblings)
  - Em trai / Em gái (younger siblings)

**Key Teaching Point:** Vietnamese distinguishes between:
- Older vs. younger siblings (not just "brother/sister")
- Age hierarchy is very important in Vietnamese culture

**Model Sentences:**
```
Teacher's family example:
"Gia đình tôi có bốn người."
(My family has four people.)

"Tôi có một anh trai và một em gái."
(I have one older brother and one younger sister.)

"Bố tôi tên là [name]."
(My father's name is [name].)
```

**Extended Family:**
- Briefly introduce grandparents: ông, bà
- Mention aunts and uncles exist (will learn details later)

**Cultural Note:** Vietnamese family structures are often extended family living together or very close by. Family is central to Vietnamese culture.

#### 3. Pronunciation Practice (15 min)

**Activity 1: "Family Member Drill"**
- Teacher models each family term
- Students repeat chorally (3 times each)
- Special attention to:
  - Gia đình (family - falling tones)
  - Anh trai (older brother)
  - Chị gái (older sister)

**Activity 2: "Older vs. Younger"**
- Practice distinguishing:
  - Anh trai (older brother) vs. Em trai (younger brother)
  - Chị gái (older sister) vs. Em gái (younger sister)
- Pair practice: Student A says one, Student B identifies "older" or "younger"

**Tricky Sounds:**
- "Chị" - practice the "ch" sound
- "Gái" - proper tone (high rising)
- "Của" - the "ư" vowel sound

**Materials:** Pronunciation guide, audio examples

#### 4. Guided Practice: Describing Your Family (25 min)

**Activity 1: "Family Tree Drawing"**
- Provide blank family tree template
- Students fill in with their own family (can use English names)
- Label each person in Vietnamese (bố, mẹ, etc.)
- Write Vietnamese vocabulary terms next to each person

**Activity 2: "My Family Composition"**
- Teach structure: "Gia đình tôi có [số người] người"
  (My family has [number] people)
- Students write their sentence
- Practice saying it aloud
- Teacher circulates, corrects

**Activity 3: "Family Members I Have"**
- Teach: "Tôi có [number] [family member]"
- Students create 3-5 sentences about their family
- Example:
  ```
  Tôi có một anh trai.
  Tôi có một em gái.
  Tôi không có em trai.
  ```

**Activity 4: "Possessive Practice"**
- Practice: của tôi (my/mine)
- Drill sentences:
  - Gia đình của tôi (my family)
  - Bố của tôi (my father)
  - Em gái của tôi (my younger sister)
- Note: Often "của" is dropped: gia đình tôi

**Materials:** Family tree templates, writing materials, example sentences

#### 5. Communicative Practice: Family Information Exchange (30 min)

**Activity 1: "Family Size Survey"**
- Students walk around class
- Ask 5 classmates: "Gia đình bạn có mấy người?" (How many people in your family?)
- Record answers
- Calculate class average
- Practice numbers!

**Activity 2: "Family Interviews"**
- Pair students
- Each asks partner about family:
  ```
  Q: Bạn có anh trai không? (Do you have older brother?)
  A: Có, tôi có một anh trai. / Không, tôi không có anh trai.
  
  Q: Bạn có em gái không?
  A: Có, tôi có hai em gái. / Không.
  ```
- Switch roles
- Share interesting findings with class

**Activity 3: "Family Photo Share"**
- Students bring family photo (or describe from memory)
- In small groups (3-4 people)
- Show photo and describe family members:
  ```
  "Đây là gia đình tôi." (This is my family)
  "Đây là bố tôi." (This is my father)
  "Đây là mẹ tôi." (This is my mother)
  "Tôi có một anh trai. Tên anh ấy là [name]."
  ```

**Activity 4: "Find Someone Who..."**
- Create bingo-style worksheet:
  - Find someone who has an older brother
  - Find someone who has two younger sisters
  - Find someone who has grandparents living with them
  - Find someone who is an only child
- Students mingle, ask questions in Vietnamese, record names

**Materials:** Survey sheets, interview question cards, photos/descriptions

#### 6. Application Activity: Family Presentations (10 min)

**Activity: "Meet My Family"**
- Each student presents their family to the class (1-2 minutes)
- Use family tree as visual aid
- Required elements:
  - Family size: Gia đình tôi có ___ người
  - Family members: Tôi có...
  - At least 3 family member descriptions
  - Use possessive structure at least once

**Presentation Structure:**
```
Xin chào!
Gia đình tôi có [number] người.
Tôi có [list family members].
Bố tôi tên là [name].
Mẹ tôi tên là [name].
[Continue...]
Cảm ơn!
```

**Assessment:** Teacher uses simple checklist for completion

**Materials:** Family trees (completed), presentation checklist

#### 7. Wrap-up & Homework (5 min)

**Review Game: "Family Member Popcorn"**
- Teacher calls out English family term
- Student called stands and says Vietnamese term
- That student then says another English term
- Another student responds
- Fast pace!

**Homework Assignment:**

1. **Family Description Essay (30 min):**
   - Write 10-12 sentences about your family in Vietnamese
   - Include: family size, each member, their names, ages (optional)
   - Use possessive structures
   - Bring to class for peer review

2. **Vocabulary Practice (20 min):**
   - Create flashcards for all Week 3 family vocabulary
   - Practice with app or physical cards
   - Record yourself saying each term

3. **Family Tree Poster (30 min):**
   - Create a detailed family tree with Vietnamese labels
   - Include photos or drawings
   - Add ages, occupations (in English if needed)
   - Be creative! This will be displayed in class

4. **Listening Practice (15 min):**
   - Listen to Week 3 audio files (3 times)
   - Try to write what you hear

5. **Cultural Reading (20 min):**
   - Read: "Vietnamese Family Structure and Values"
   - Write: Compare Vietnamese family culture to your own (3-5 sentences in English)

6. **Preparation:**
   - Review Weeks 1-3 vocabulary
   - Week 4 quiz will cover all three weeks!

**Next Week Preview:** Numbers, Time & Calendar - we'll learn to tell time and talk about schedules!

### Assessment

**Formative:**
- Vocabulary quiz (Week 1-2): 5-10 minutes
- Monitor pronunciation during drills
- Check family tree accuracy
- Observe interview interactions
- Note presentation completeness

**Exit Ticket:**
Each student must tell teacher:
1. How many people in their family (Vietnamese)
2. One family member they have (Vietnamese)
3. Say goodbye!

### Teacher Notes

**Key Teaching Points:**

1. **Age Hierarchy is Crucial:** Vietnamese distinguishes older/younger siblings because age determines pronouns used. This is fundamental to Vietnamese social structure.

2. **Extended Family:** Vietnamese families are often large and close. Don't assume nuclear family structure.

3. **Pronunciation Challenges:**
   - "Chị" and "gái" - practice extensively
   - "Của" - the ư sound is difficult for English speakers

4. **Sensitivity:** Some students may have complicated family situations - make participation optional for personal details.

**Common Errors:**
- Confusing anh trai and em trai (older vs. younger brother)
- Forgetting classifiers with numbers
- Mispronouncing "gia đình"

**Differentiation:**
- **Extra Support:** Provide completed family tree example, sentence frames
- **Challenge:** Include extended family, ages, occupations in Vietnamese

**Time Management:**
- If running long, reduce "Find Someone Who" game
- Application activity can be homework if needed

### Cultural Notes to Share

**Vietnamese Family Values:**
- Family is the center of society
- Respect for elders is paramount
- Children often live with parents until marriage (sometimes after!)
- Grandparents often help raise grandchildren
- Family decisions are collective, not individual

**Kinship Terms Complexity:**
- Vietnamese has MANY specific terms for relatives
- Terms depend on: paternal vs. maternal side, older vs. younger than parent, gender
- We're learning simplified version for beginners
- This complexity reflects how important family relationships are

**Interesting Facts:**
- Most Vietnamese can recite their family tree back many generations
- Ancestor worship is common - families maintain altars
- Tết (Lunar New Year) is all about family reunions

### Success Indicators

Students successfully completed Week 3 if they can:
- ✓ Name at least 5 family member terms
- ✓ Describe their family size in Vietnamese
- ✓ Use "có" (have) correctly
- ✓ Distinguish older vs. younger siblings
- ✓ Complete basic family presentation
- ✓ Understand importance of age hierarchy
