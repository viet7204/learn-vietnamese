## WEEK 3: Family & Relationships

### Learning Objectives
- Describe family members and relationships
- Use possessive structures (của, của tôi)
- Count family members
- Understand kinship terms (complex but start simple)
- Talk about marital status

### Key Vocabulary (40-50 words)
- Gia đình - Family
- Bố/Ba - Father
- Mẹ/Ma - Mother
- Anh trai - Older brother
- Chị gái - Older sister
- Em trai - Younger brother
- Em gái - Younger sister
- Con trai - Son
- Con gái - Daughter
- Vợ - Wife
- Chồng - Husband
- Ông - Grandfather
- Bà - Grandmother
- Bạn - Friend
- Người yêu - Boyfriend/Girlfriend

### Grammar Focus
- Possessive: của + pronoun (của tôi = my, của bạn = your)
- Có (have/has): Tôi có hai em gái (I have two younger sisters)
- Classifier: Người (for people)

### Activities
- Family tree creation and presentation
- "Who is this?" photo sharing game
- Family size survey
- Age ordering activity (eldest to youngest)

### Homework
- Draw family tree with Vietnamese labels
- Write 5 sentences about family
- Memorize immediate family members' terms
- Quiz preparation (Weeks 1-3)

### Assessment
- **Week 3 Quiz:** Vocabulary from Weeks 1-2 (5-10 min)
- Family presentation (informal)

## WEEK 4: Numbers, Time & Calendar

### Learning Objectives
- Count to 100
- Tell time (hours and minutes)
- Express dates, days of week, months
- Make appointments/plans
- Ask "What time is it?"
- Talk about daily schedule

### Key Vocabulary (40-50 words)
- Giờ - Hour/Time
- Phút - Minute
- Mấy giờ? - What time?
- Ngày - Day
- Tuần - Week
- Tháng - Month
- Năm - Year
- Days: Thứ Hai (Monday) through Chủ Nhật (Sunday)
- Months: Tháng Một (January) through Tháng Mười Hai (December)
- Hôm nay - Today
- Ngày mai - Tomorrow
- Hôm qua - Yesterday

### Grammar Focus
- Time expressions: lúc (at time), vào (at general time)
- "Bây giờ là mấy giờ?" (What time is it now?)
- Numbers 21-100
- Ordinal numbers with dates

### Activities
- Clock practice (analog and digital)
- Schedule making
- Birthday survey
- "What time do you..." daily routine intro
- Calendar bingo

### Homework
- Create weekly schedule in Vietnamese
- Practice telling time (use online tool)
- Memorize days and months
- Study for Mid-term Assessment (Week 4)

### Assessment
- **Week 4 Mid-term Progress Check:**
  - Oral interview (greetings, introduction, family Q&A)
  - Written: Vocabulary test (Weeks 1-4)
  - Listening: Identify times and dates
  - Self-reflection: What's challenging? What's going well?

## WEEK 5: Food & Dining

### Learning Objectives
- Order food at restaurant
- Express food preferences (like/dislike)
- Name common Vietnamese dishes
- Request bill, say thank you
- Describe taste (delicious, spicy, sweet, etc.)
- Basic restaurant etiquette

### Key Vocabulary (50-60 words)
- Ăn - To eat
- Uống - To drink
- Đói - Hungry
- Khát - Thirsty
- Ngon - Delicious
- Thích - To like
- Không thích - Don't like
- Muốn - Want

**Food Items:**
- Cơm - Rice
- Phở - Vietnamese noodle soup
- Bánh mì - Vietnamese sandwich
- Bún - Rice vermicelli
- Nước - Water
- Cà phê - Coffee
- Trà - Tea
- Bia - Beer

**Restaurant Words:**
- Nhà hàng - Restaurant
- Thực đơn - Menu
- Tính tiền - Bill/Check
- Bao nhiêu tiền? - How much?

### Grammar Focus
- "Tôi muốn..." (I want...)
- "Cho tôi..." (Give me.../I'll have...)
- Measure words: ly (glass), bát (bowl), đĩa (plate)
- Negation: Không (not)

### Activities
- Menu reading practice
- Restaurant role-play
- Food preference survey
- "Vietnamese Food Show and Tell"
- Order-taking game

### Cultural Notes
- Vietnamese dining etiquette
- Chopstick use
- How to address restaurant staff
- Tipping culture in Vietnam.

### Homework
- Create favorite meal menu in Vietnamese
- Visit Vietnamese restaurant (optional but encouraged!)
- Food vocabulary flashcards
- Watch: Vietnamese cooking video

## WEEK 6: Shopping & Money

### Learning Objectives
- Negotiate prices at market
- Ask "How much is this?"
- Count money (Vietnamese đồng)
- Describe clothing and items
- Express size, color preferences
- Haggling basics

### Key Vocabulary (50-60 words)
- Mua - Buy
- Bán - Sell
- Tiền - Money
- Đồng - Vietnamese currency (dong)
- Bao nhiêu? - How much?
- Đắt - Expensive
- Rẻ - Cheap
- Giảm giá - Discount
- Colors: Đỏ (red), Xanh (blue/green), Vàng (yellow), Đen (black), Trắng (white)
- Sizes: Nhỏ (small), Vừa (medium), Lớn (large)

**Items:**
- Áo - Shirt
- Quần - Pants
- Giày - Shoes
- Túi - Bag
- Sách - Book

### Grammar Focus
- Classifier system (cái for objects, quyển for books)
- "Cái này/cái kia" (this one/that one)
- Comparison: Hơn (more than)

### Activities
- Market simulation
- Price tag reading
- Bargaining role-plays
- Shopping list creation
- Color matching game

### Homework
- Create shopping list with prices
- Practice money counting
- Research: Vietnamese markets
- Color flashcards

- Review all major topics (Weeks 1-11)

- Greetings and introductions

- Numbers, time, dates

- Daily routines

### Activities

**Review Games:**
- Vietnamese Jeopardy (categories by week)
- Pronunciation Olympics
- Vocabulary relay race

**Final Project Presentations:**
- 5-minute oral presentation
- Students choose topic: introduce yourself comprehensively, describe perfect day in Vietnam, family and lifestyle, or create own

### Assessment - FINAL EVALUATION

**1. Oral Presentation (20 points)**
- 5-minute prepared presentation
- Clear pronunciation and tone accuracy
- Vocabulary range
- Confidence and fluency

**2. Conversation Assessment (20 points)**
- One-on-one conversation with teacher
- Random topics from course
- Natural interaction
- Ability to ask and answer questions

**3. Listening Comprehension (15 points)**
- Listen to dialogues and answer questions
- Multiple choice and short answer
- Topics from Weeks 1-12

**4. Reading Comprehension (15 points)**
- Read short passages
- Answer comprehension questions
- Demonstrate vocabulary understanding

**5. Written Portfolio (15 points)**
- Collection of best work from semester
- Minimum 5 pieces (introductions, routines, descriptions)
- Self-reflection essay in English
- Progress documentation

**6. Class Participation (15 points)**
- Attendance and engagement throughout course
- Homework completion
- Class contributions
- Growth mindset

**Total: 100 points**

### Celebration & Closure

## Teaching Tips Across All Weeks

### General Strategies:

1. **Tone Consistency:** Practice tones every class (warm-up)
2. **Spiral Curriculum:** Review previous material regularly
3. **Real-World Application:** Use authentic materials when possible
4. **Cultural Integration:** Weave culture throughout, not just add-ons
5. **Student Autonomy:** Encourage self-study and independence
6. **Error Correction:** Balance correction with encouragement
7. **Differentiation:** Provide extensions and support as needed
8. **Engagement:** Keep energy high, use variety of activities

### Adult Learner Considerations:

- Respect time constraints and commitments
- Connect to students' purposes for learning
- Leverage life experience
- Provide rationale for activities
- Allow for self-direction
- Create safe, respectful environment
- Recognize different learning speeds

### Success Factors:

- Clear objectives each week
- Consistent structure (familiar routine)
- Immediate feedback
- Celebration of progress
- Community building
- Practical, applicable content
- Multimodal learning (visual, auditory, kinesthetic)
