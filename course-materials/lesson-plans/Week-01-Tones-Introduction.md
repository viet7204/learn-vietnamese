# Week 1: Introduction & Vietnamese Tones
## Lesson Plan - Adult Beginners Course

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Recognize and produce all six Vietnamese tones
2. Understand the importance of tones in Vietnamese
3. Pronounce basic Vietnamese consonants and vowels
4. Say simple greetings: Xin chào (Hello), Tạm biệt (Goodbye)
5. Count from 0-10 in Vietnamese
6. Introduce the concept of personal pronouns (basic)

### Vocabulary (20-30 words)

**Core Vocabulary:**
- Xin chào - Hello
- Tạm biệt - Goodbye
- Cảm ơn - Thank you
- Không - No
- Có - Yes
- Vâng - Yes (polite)
- Dạ - Yes (polite, softer)
- Xin lỗi - Sorry/Excuse me
- Chào buổi sáng - Good morning
- Chào buổi chiều - Good afternoon

**Numbers 0-10:**
- Không/Zéro (0), Một (1), Hai (2), Ba (3), Bốn (4), Năm (5), Sáu (6), Bảy (7), Tám (8), Chín (9), Mười (10)

**Classroom Language:**
- Giáo viên - Teacher
- Học sinh - Student
- Lớp học - Class

### Grammar Focus

- Introduction to word order (Subject-Verb-Object, similar to English)
- No verb conjugation in Vietnamese
- Tones change meaning completely

### Lesson Plan (120 minutes)

#### 1. Warm-up & Introduction (15 min)

**Activity: "Hello in Many Languages"**
- Ask students to share "hello" in languages they know
- Introduce Vietnamese: "Xin chào" (pronounced: sin chow)
- Brief introduction: Why learn Vietnamese? Student motivations
- Course overview and expectations

**Materials:** World map, question prompts

#### 2. Presentation: Vietnamese Tones (20 min)

**The Six Tones:**

Demonstrate each tone using the syllable "ma":

1. **Mid-level (no mark):** ma - ghost (→)
2. **High rising (á):** má - mother (↗)
3. **Low falling (à):** mà - but (↘)
4. **Rising (ả):** mả - tomb (↗↘ curve up then down)
5. **Falling-rising (ã):** mã - horse (↘↗ dip down then up)
6. **Low falling-rising (ạ):** mạ - rice seedling (↓ low drop)

**Visual Aids:**
- Hand gestures for each tone (establish consistent gestures)
- Tone chart on board with arrows showing pitch movement
- Audio examples (clear, slow pronunciation)

**Key Teaching Point:** "Tones aren't 'accents' - they change the meaning entirely!"

**Cultural Note:** Explain that Vietnamese is a tonal language, like Chinese, and tones are essential for communication.

#### 3. Pronunciation Practice: Tones (15 min)

**Activity 1: "Tone Echoes"**
- Teacher says syllable with tone, students echo
- Practice with "ma" family
- Introduce other common syllables: ba, ca, da, la
- Exaggerate tones at first, then normalize

**Activity 2: "Tone Hearing"**
- Play audio of pairs (má vs. mà)
- Students identify which tone they hear (thumbs up for first, thumbs down for second)
- Start slowly, gradually increase speed

**Mini-Activity: "Tone Song"**
Create a simple melody using the tones with "ma ma ma má mà..."

**Materials:** Audio files, tone charts, hand gesture guide

#### 4. Guided Practice: Basic Greetings (25 min)

**Activity 1: "Xin chào!" Practice**
- Model: "Xin chào" with proper tone (chào uses ̀ low falling tone)
- Students repeat chorally, then individually
- Practice with emphasis on the falling tone of "chào"

**Activity 2: "Greeting Circle"**
- Students stand in circle
- Each person turns to neighbor and says "Xin chào"
- Neighbor responds "Xin chào" then turns to next person
- Continue around circle twice for rhythm

**Activity 3: "Hello & Goodbye Role-play"**
- Teach: Tạm biệt (Goodbye)
- Students walk around room
- When they meet someone: greet, chat briefly in English, say goodbye in Vietnamese
- Rotate partners 4-5 times

**Introduce Formality:**
- Xin chào - standard hello
- Chào + [title/pronoun] - more specific (will expand later)

**Materials:** Circle formation space, role-play cards

#### 5. Communicative Practice: Numbers & Counting Games (30 min)

**Activity 1: "Numbers 0-10 Introduction"**
- Present numbers with visual aids (fingers, number cards)
- Model pronunciation with tones
- Special attention to: Không (0), Năm (5 - rising tone), Mười (10)

**Pronunciation Focus:**
- Một (one): mid-level tone
- Hai (two): mid-tone
- Năm (five): rising tone (ã mark)

**Activity 2: "Number Chain"**
- Students stand in line
- First person says "Một" (1)
- Next says "Hai" (2), continuing to 10
- When reach 10, reverse back to 0
- Speed up gradually

**Activity 3: "Finger Flash"**
- Teacher shows fingers (1-10)
- Students call out number in Vietnamese
- Reverse: Teacher says number, students show fingers
- Add competition element: split into teams

**Activity 4: "Number Bingo"**
- Create simple 3x3 bingo cards with numbers 1-10
- Teacher calls numbers in Vietnamese
- Students mark their cards
- Winner shouts "Xin chào!"

**Materials:** Number flashcards, bingo cards (prepared), markers

#### 6. Application Activity: Introduction Exchange (10 min)

**Activity: "My First Vietnamese Words"**
- Students practice mini-dialogue:
  - Person A: "Xin chào!"
  - Person B: "Xin chào!"
  - Person A: "Một, hai, ba..." (counts to 5)
  - Person B: counts to 5 in response
  - Both: "Tạm biệt!"

- Walk around, find 3 different partners
- Perform exchange with each
- Focus on correct tone pronunciation

**Materials:** Conversation prompt card (visual reminder)

#### 7. Wrap-up & Homework Assignment (5 min)

**Review:**
- Quick tone review using hand gestures
- Choral repetition: "Xin chào" and "Tạm biệt"
- Count 0-10 together

**Homework Assignment:**

1. **Tone Practice (15 min daily):**
   - Listen to Week 1 audio files (3-5 times)
   - Practice "ma" with all six tones
   - Record yourself saying the six tones (use phone)
   - Compare with model audio

2. **Number Practice (10 min):**
   - Practice counting 0-10 aloud 5 times
   - Count random objects in your home (in Vietnamese)
   - Write out numbers 0-10 five times each

3. **Greeting Practice (5 min):**
   - Practice "Xin chào" and "Tạm biệt" 20 times each
   - Greet yourself in the mirror (tone practice!)

4. **Cultural Exploration (20 min):**
   - Watch: "Introduction to Vietnamese Language" video (link provided)
   - Read: "Why Are There Tones in Vietnamese?" article (handout)
   - Write: 3 sentences about what you learned

5. **Preparation:**
   - Bring a family photo or be ready to describe your family (we'll use Week 2-3)

**Next Week Preview:** Greetings & Self-Introduction - we'll learn to introduce ourselves!

### Assessment

**Formative Assessment:**
- Monitor tone production during practice activities
- Check understanding through "Tone Hearing" activity responses
- Observe participation in greeting exchanges
- Note: This is Week 1, focus on effort and enthusiasm, not perfection!

**Exit Ticket:**
- As students leave, each must:
  1. Greet teacher: "Xin chào!"
  2. Count to 5 in Vietnamese
  3. Say goodbye: "Tạm biệt!"

### Teacher Notes

**Important Reminders:**

1. **Be Patient with Tones:** Adult learners often struggle initially with tones. Emphasize this is normal!
2. **Use Hand Gestures:** Establish consistent hand gestures for each tone from Day 1
3. **Exaggerate at First:** Model tones with exaggeration, students will naturally moderate
4. **Create Safe Space:** Emphasize mistakes are part of learning - create encouraging environment
5. **Energy Level:** Keep energy high - this is Week 1, set positive tone!
6. **Individual Differences:** Some students pick up tones quickly, others need more time

**Common Issues:**

- **Tone Confusion:** English speakers often conflate tones - lots of drilling needed
- **Rising vs. Rising-falling:** These are easily confused - extra practice
- **Self-consciousness:** Adults may feel embarrassed - group practice helps!

**Time Management:**
- If running short on time, reduce bingo game
- Tone practice is most important - prioritize this section
- Application activity can be reduced to 5 minutes if needed

**Differentiation:**

- **Struggling Students:** 
  - Pair with stronger students during activities
  - Provide extra tone chart handout
  - Offer to record additional practice audio

- **Advanced Students:**
  - Invite them to help demonstrate
  - Challenge with numbers 11-20
  - Ask them to teach one tone to a partner

### Cultural Notes to Share

- Vietnamese has three main dialects: Northern (Hanoi), Central (Huế), and Southern (Saigon). This course focuses on Northern dialect as it's the standard.
- Greetings in Vietnamese depend on the relationship and age difference - we'll explore this more in Week 2
- Vietnamese uses Latin alphabet (quốc ngữ) - easier for English speakers than Chinese characters!
- Tone mistakes can lead to funny misunderstandings - share example: "cá" (fish) vs "cà" (eggplant)
